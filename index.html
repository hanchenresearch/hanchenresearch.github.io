Biography

I am currently a fourth year PhD candidate. My research interests are artificial intelligence, recommender systems, human-computer interaction, digital advertising, price promotion, consumer decision making, and behavioral economics. Methodologically, I employ machine learning, econometrics, field and lab experiments. I am interested in how firms develop effective marketing tools (such as marketing promotions and product recommendations) to influence consumer behavior in the era of artificial intelligence to benefit for the good of both consumers and firms. I have a special focus on sequential marketing treatment policy evaluation.
